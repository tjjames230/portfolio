Project Overview

The idea for myLibrary came from the need to organize all of the books, articles and blog posts I had bought and saved over the years. I wanted to have one location I could search that would tell me where I kept the literature, physical or digital.

Problem Statement

Users do not have an easy way to keep track of all their reading material.

Target Users

A typical user would be on the app to add their books and other reading materials, or to look up a previously added material to find the progress, page location, reviews and additional info.

Sketches & Brainstorming

I began by laying out ideas for the home page, viewing more details, and the ability to add books and articles. I wanted users to be able to add their own notes to really personalize their collection.

Wireframes

I cleaned up the sketches and moved to a digital format. This helped me visualize the app more accurately, while still allowing room for changes.

Mockups

With a more concrete vision in mind for the app, I began creating the high-fidelity mockups. Some of the overall layout shifted, but more importantly, I was able to expand on a lot of the brainstorming I had done earlier.

Conclusion

Overall I learned a lot from this project. If I were to redo this app I would probably do more research earlier in the process. A lot of the decisions I had made were already based on an idea I had for the design. I would also want to try and get more feedback or perform some usability tests to figure out what are some pain points.
