Project Overview

The idea for Coffee Time came about when I was wanting to find a local coffee shop, but enjoyed the ease-of-use from Starbucks' and Caribou's apps. With an app like this, everyone would be able to connect over their love of coffee and tea.

Problem Statement

Users are limited to one coffee app per chain and don't have any apps for local coffee shops.

Target Users

A typical user is likely going to be younger in age. They would be on the app to order drinks, see what other people are drinking, share recipes, and find shops.

Sketches & Brainstorming

I was pretty loose and exploratory during my sketch phase. I roughly laid out a general idea of how I wanted the app to function and some of the main features I wanted to focus on, such as seeing profiles, messages and the main feed that would feature all of the drinks people were sharing.

Wireframes

When I was building out the wireframes I got a better sense of spacing and layout. I also thought of featuring a drink from a specific local shop.

Mockups

With all of the layouts and features completed, I created the finalized mockups. These expanded upon the wireframes and helped show me areas I needed to adjust.

Conclusion

While I learned a lot from this project, it taught me the importance of planning a project as if I were the one developing it as well. I wasn't entirely sure at the time how all of these stores would connect and I think that would be one of the biggest challenges if I attempted to build this app. The other drawback from an app like this is people can already share drinks on the general social media apps, so I would have to be able to cater more towards folks that only want to see drinks and local coffee shops.
